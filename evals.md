# Evals

Binary yes/no checks that define what "good" looks like. Max 6 total.
The first 3 are universal defaults. Add your own domain-specific evals below.

---

EVAL 1: Consciousness floor
Question: Is this content free of fear-mongering, shame, blame, guilt-tripping, or manipulation tactics?
Pass: Content informs, teaches, or inspires without exploiting negative emotions
Fail: Content uses fear, shame, or manufactured urgency to drive engagement
NOTE: Hard floor — if this fails, the entire score is 0 regardless of other evals

EVAL 2: Genuine value
Question: Does this content teach, inform, or genuinely help the target reader in a concrete way?
Pass: Reader walks away with a new insight, practical takeaway, or useful perspective
Fail: Content is entertaining but empty, or informative but irrelevant to the target reader

EVAL 3: Worth existing
Question: Would the reader's day be slightly better for having read this?
Pass: Content respects the reader's time and leaves them better off
Fail: Content wastes the reader's time or adds noise to their feed

EVAL 4: Hook
Question: Does the opening line contain a specific claim, story, or question (not a generic statement)?
Pass: First sentence makes a concrete, specific assertion or asks a genuine question
Fail: Opens with "In today's world...", "Have you ever...", or other generic filler

EVAL 5: Forward motion
Question: After reading this, would the reader naturally want to learn more about the product or try it — without being explicitly told to?
Pass: Content creates genuine curiosity or demonstrates enough value that the reader self-motivates to take the next step
Fail: Content either has no connection to the product, or uses pushy CTAs like "Sign up now!" or "Don't miss out!"

EVAL 6: No slop
Question: Is the content free of these banned phrases: "game-changer", "here's the kicker", "the best part", "level up", "in today's fast-paced world", "buckle up", "let that sink in"?
Pass: Zero banned phrases found
Fail: Any banned phrase appears
